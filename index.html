The Adirondack Park is located in Upstate New York.

Fondly reffered to as the Blue Line, its boundary encompasses nearly 6 million acres, and is unique in the fact that 50% is publicly owned and the other 50% is privately owened within the Park Boundary.

At the time, over utilization of timber and water resources were a huge concern.

Many areas through upstate were completely logged, all natural resources available, were harvested to the extreme.


The Adirondack Park boasts over 3,000 lakes, 30,000 miles of rivers and streams, and a wide variety of habitats, including globally unique wetland types and old growth forests. The heart of the Adirondack Park is the Forest Preserve, which was created when Article XIV of the New York State Constitution was signed into law in 1885.
Constitutionally, the Adirondack Park, is to remain "Forever Wild".

About 123,000 people live in the park year round in 101 towns and villages.

Within this map, the Public Land and Private Land layers denote what areas are owned by state and private entities.

The Adirondacks are the largest National Historic Landmark in the United States, covering an area larger than Yellowstone, Yosemite, Grand Canyon, Glacier, and the Great Smokies National Parks combined.
